Social Networks now days

A social network is, at its core, a structure of relationships. Depending on the context, the term has two
distinct meanings: one from sociology (how humans connect) and one from technology (the apps we use).

Long before the internet, sociologists used this term to map human behavior. In this context, a social
network is a social structure made up of social actors. Elements: nodes and ties.

Social Networking Sites (SNS). These are online platforms or apps that allow people to build, maintain,
and visualize social relations with other people.

Social media has redefined human interaction and the global economy, evolving from simple communication
tools into ecosystems that shape culture and public opinion. Its impact is twofold: it democratizes information
and connects communities, but it also centralizes power and poses constant challenges to privacy and mental
well-being.
