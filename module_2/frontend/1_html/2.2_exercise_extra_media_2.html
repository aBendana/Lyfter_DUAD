Solar System Planets


Planets:
Mars

Mars is a rocky planet known for its reddish color caused by iron oxide on its surface. It has mountains, vast canyons, and polar ice caps that change with the seasons. Evidence suggests that liquid water once flowed across its terrain, leaving behind dried riverbeds and mineral deposits. Often called the "Red Planet," Mars remains one of the most studied worlds due to its potential for past life and its importance in future human exploration.


  • Approximate distance from the Sun: 227 million km
  • Rotation period: 24.6 hours
  • Number of moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)
  • Atmosphere: Yes (thin, mostly carbon dioxide)
Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Its atmosphere displays vibrant cloud bands, swirling storms, and the Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has raged for centuries. Beneath its thick atmosphere lies an immense, high-pressure interior that may contain a deep liquid metallic hydrogen layer. Jupiter's powerful gravity shapes the orbits of nearby objects and protects the inner planets by capturing or deflecting comets and asteroids.


  • Approximate distance from the Sun: 778 million km
  • Rotation period: 9.9 hours (fastest of all planets)
  • Number of moons: 95+ known
  • Atmosphere: Yes (thick, hydrogen and helium)
Saturn

Saturn is a gas giant famous for its bright and extensive ring system made of ice, rock, and dust particles that orbit the planet. Like Jupiter, it is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium and features powerful winds, swirling cloud patterns, and intense storms in its atmosphere. Saturn is home to a large collection of moons, including Titan, one of the most Earth-like bodies in the solar system with thick clouds and liquid methane lakes. Its striking appearance and complex system make Saturn one of the most fascinating planets to study.


  • Approximate distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion km
  • Rotation period: 10.7 hours
  • Number of moons: 80+ known
  • Atmosphere: Yes (hydrogen, helium, methane traces)
