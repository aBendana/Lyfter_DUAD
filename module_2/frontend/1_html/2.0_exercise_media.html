Kings of their environment


Big Predators:
Grizzly Bear

Grizzly bears are large, powerful mammals known for their distinctive hump of muscle on their shoulders and thick brown fur. They are highly adaptable and can be found in forests, mountains, and river valleys. Grizzlies are omnivores, feeding on plants, insects, fish, and small mammals. Despite their size, they can run fast and are excellent diggers and swimmers.


  • Weight: 130–360 kg (290–790 lbs)
  • Length: 198–240 cm (6.5–8 ft)
  • Main food sources: Omnivorous diet including fish (salmon), berries, roots, small mammals, and carrion
  • Lifespan: 20–25 years in the wild, up to 50 years in captivity
Barbary Lion

The Barbary lion, once native to North Africa, is known for its impressive size and dark, full mane that extended over the shoulders and belly. These lions were powerful predators that lived in forests and mountainous regions. Although now extinct in the wild (still in zoos), they are remembered as one of the largest lion subspecies ever recorded.


  • Weight: 180–270 kg (400–600 lbs) for males
  • Length: 200–280 cm (6.5–9 ft), including tail
  • Main food sources: Large herbivores such as gazelles, deer, boars, and sometimes livestock
  • Lifespan: 15–18 years in the wild; up to 25 years in captivity
Great White Shark

The great white shark is a powerful marine predator known for its size, torpedo-shaped body, and rows of sharp, replaceable teeth. It inhabits coastal and offshore waters around the world, particularly in cool temperate regions. Great whites are fast swimmers and rely on their acute senses to locate prey over long distances.


  • Weight: 520–1,100 kg (1,150–2,430 lbs)
  • Length: 400–600 cm (13–20 ft)
  • Main food sources: Fish, seals, sea lions, dolphins, and carrion
  • Lifespan: 30–70 years
